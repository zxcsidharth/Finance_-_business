## <u>Terminologies</u>

***Book value vs Market value/current price:-***
Book value matlab ki kisi share ka original price kya hoga agar uske sare assets ko calculate kar k uska price nikala jaye to kya hoga..wo jo price hoga uss price ko total share se divide kar denge to book value mill jata hai. But ye nikal pana bahut difficult hota hai for individual investor so ye info companies hi jyadatar case me reveal karti hai.

NOTE:- agar kabhi kisi share ka market value agar book value k pass me ya usse kam hai to uss case me wo share acha return de sakta hai agar uske baki sab finances and analysis sahi hai to. But yad rhe ki uske sare finances agar thik ho tabhi le.. warna ye v ho sakta hai ki loss k wajah se company k share gir gaye ho aur wo company ka sahre obviously book value se kam hi ho jayega.
Also jo book value since assets k upar calculate hota hai, to uss case me jo assets ka price hota hai wo uss assets ka kharida hua hi price k upar hi book value calculate hota hai, jabki assets v badhte hain time k sath. Like agar koi company ne 100 sal pehle land kharida hoga to book value uss 100 sal pehle k land k price pr calculate hoga, jab ki abhi uss land ka keemat sayad bahut jyada ho sakta hai. so remember this also.

***P/E Ratio:-***
we can find this ratio for stock as well as for the company.
stock (price/earning) = (price per share/ earning per share in one year)
for company (P/E) = total market cap / earning per year

for example for stocks:-

let's say one share has a price of 100 rs and in one year it gave a profit of 20 rs, 
so P/E = 100/20 = 5. it means that in 5 year if this stock gives same kind of return then it will gets double. 
P/E ratio ko payback time v kehte hain it means ki jitna humne lagaya tha utna hmko return kitne time me aa jayega.
***NOTE***:- Ye indicator trading k liye nai hai, ye indicator use karna important hota hai for long term investing.

So we should look for the **EPS** which is know as the earnings per share. to agar kisi stock ka EPS let's say 70 hai to iska matlab ki ek stock pr pichle ek sal me 70 rupay ka profit hua hai agar uss stock to sahi price pr kharida gaya ho to, agar high price pr koi kharidega to obviously utna profit nai millega.

## <u>Market Capitalisation</ul>

Matlab uss company k total assets including cashflow and assets. ye do tarah k hote hain 
1. ***CONSOLIDATED***:- matlab ki uski kuch subsidiary company v hongi unn sabka mila kar jo capital hoga wo hoga iss case me MC.
2. ***STANDALONE***:- Matlab excluding the subsidiary companies. bss purely usi ka capitalisation ka baat ho.

Formula for MC = NO. of share * Latest traded price;

PS:- PEERS ka matlab hota hai ki usi fields ki companies.

**categorisation**:-based on the market cap this is divided into three parts.
1. ***LARGE CAP***:- Market cap >= 10000 cr.
2. ***MID CAP***:- 500cr >= MC <= 10000 cr.
3. ***SMALL CAP***:- MC < 500.

***FREE FLOAT***:-  **Kai baar hum dekhenge ki Market Cap k baju me Free float v likha hota hai? What is that?**
Let's understand this with an example, that lets say a company has 100000 shares but out of 10 lakh the promoters holds the 6 lakh shares, so the remaining 4 lakhs would be here to trade for the retail investeor, Institutional Investor and HNI (high networth individual) 

so Free float is  = (total Shares - Promoters holding) * price per share.

so ye ho jayega free float and agar iko minus kar denge market cap se to promoters ka holding v pata chal jayega.

So now agar ek company me agar founders/promoters ka share acha khasa hai to iska matlab ki company k growth pr ya founders pr bharosa kara jaa sakta hai.

## <u>Profit & loss analysis</ul>

- This shows the health of business operations
- Shows you a trend in business cycle
- Helps in future price target calculation
- Basically ye bata dega ki company growth story me hai ya downward growth me hai.
#### <font style=color:magenta>SALES</font>
Total revenue earned from core business operations.

**GOOD Sign:-**
- Is sales **growing over time**?
- Is growth **consistent**, not random?
- Compare:
    - 3-year CAGR
    - 5-year CAGR
    - TTM growth

 **RED flags:-**
- Falling sales
- Highly volatile sales
- Growth only due to inflation
	- **But how to know if growth is happening due to inflation only?**
		- Simple hai ki agar kisi company ka sales nai badha, ya bahut kam badha  aur uske comparison me uska profit jyada badh gaya to iska matlab hai ki company ne same hi maal becha hai but iss bar price badha diya hai to uska profit badh gaya hai, ab ye chij long term me thik nai hota hai.
		- To isse kya ho gaya ki sales ka number nai badha lekin revenue badh gaya because har sal 6-7% ka usme inflation v to judd raha hai.
	- ***How to catch this with data?***
		- Sabse pehle to ye ki revenue badha hai to matlab hai ki expense v badhega because raw matterial ka v kharcha to badha hoga na, eve if company ne apna sale ka price badha diya.
		  to wo pata chalega OPM % se, agar ye badh raha hai to matlab hai ki sahi hai. ya operating profit v dekh lo ki wo badha hai ki nai.. agar wo flat hai ya ghat gaya hai to matlab hai ki company inflation ka fayda utha kar apna sales badha rhi hai.
		- Ideally hona ye chahiyeki percentage k terms me Jo profit should be greater than Sales, and sales should be greater than inflation. this below formula.
```
Profit(Net profit)CAGR > Sales CAGR > Inflation
AND
OPM is stable or improving
```
#### <font style=color:magenta>EXPENSES</font>
This includes Raw matterial cost, employee cost, manufacturing cost, employee cost( includes salary, PF, esops, other benefits), finance cost jo business chalane k liye chahiye etc. Ye badhte rehna chahiye kyunki isse ye pata chalta hai ki company apna manufacturing badha rhi hain tabhi to ye badh raha hai, nai badhe to matlab hai ki company ka production nai badh raha hai.
#### <font style=color:magenta>OPERATING COST/PROFIT</font>
- Total Sales - Expenses = Operating profit. 
- Simple bhasa me kahe to agar company ka sales me se kya kya kharce hue nikal denge to operating cost ya profit ho gaya. 
#### <font style=color:magenta>OPM</font>
- It is Operating profit margin, and can be calculated with this formula:-
  ```
  (operating cost/sales) * 100
  ```
- Agar sales badha hai aur OPM nai badh raha hai ya Operating cost nai badh raha hai to matlab hai ki company k expenses badh gaye hain and that because of inflation like raw matterial mehenge ho gaye, ya employee cost badh gaya etc.
- To OPM ka badhna bahut jaruri hai. aur iske liye company ka operating profit badhna chahiye and sales v.
  
**GOOD Sign**:- 
- Increasing OPM is good sign and ye bahut jyada fluctuate nai hone chahiye
- 10-15% is Descent, 20-30% is Strong and above 30% is Excellent company
  
**RED Flag:-**
- Negative margins
- Sharp ups and downs
#### <font style=color:magenta>OTHER INCOME</font>
- Income **not from core business**
- this includes company earnings from bonds kharidne, ya equity wagaira kahin pr kharide, rent amenities, FD interest, ya currency exchange gain ya fir company ne FD se paise nikale hain to wo v isme aata hai. etc. 
  
**Red flag:-**
- Company profitable only because of other income. agar company ka core segment ka revenue nai badh raha hai aur bajaye uske unka other income badh raha hai to matlab company apne track se hat kar paise kama rhi hain aur dusre business k bharose hi chal rhi hai.
#### <font style=color:magenta>INTEREST</font>
- Ye earning nai hai, ye hota hai jo intrest loan k liye de rhi companies, ya bonds issue kare honge humne ya debenture diye honge. ye tamam kharcho ko interest me count karte hain.
- To ye kam speed se badhe ya reducing hotarhe time k sath to bahut badhiya hota hai, it means ki aapka loan pr intrest kam jaa raha hai, jo kamai hai vohi company k vikash me lag raha hai.
**GOOD Sign:-**
- Interest < 20% of operating profit
- Interest decreasing YoY.
**RED Flag:-**
- Interest > operating profit  
    ‚Üí company borrowing just to survive.
    
#### <font style=color:magenta>DEPRICIATION/AMORTIZATION</font>
- this is applicable on the assets.
- Assets can be physical or non-physical.
- Physical assets is also called as **Tangible assets**
- Non-physical assets is also called as **Intangible assets** like IP rights, softwares, trademarks etc.
- So **Depreciation** is for tangible assets and **Ammortization** is for non-tangible assets..
- Har company k kuch kuch assets aise hote hain ki wo depreciate karte hain over the time like the physical assest(Machinery, raw material end products, vehiclese, buildings), to wo v data present hota hai har sal ka, aur agar company grow karte jaa rhi hai to Depreciation v badhega hi because company k resources v to badh rhe hain. 
- And depreciation k badhne se koi dikkat nai hai unless and untill company ka profit v agar badh raha hai to. problem tab hai jab company ka profit nai badh raha hai but depreciation badhta jaa raha hai and profit nai badh raha hai .. then iska matlab company me kuch serious issues hain.
#### <font style=color:magenta>TAX %</font>
Its simple tax kitna percent gaya hai total income - expense k amount ka.

**RED flags:-**
- Very high or negative tax
- Abnormally low tax for many years
#### <font style=color:magenta>NET PROFIT</font>
matlab kii total income se expense ko hata kar  aur fir Tax ka amount ko hata kar jo bachega wohi Net profit hua.  so formula kuch aisa hua:- 

```maths
Net profit = total income - expense - Tax amount
```
ISI ko **PROFIT AFTER TAX** v bolte hain.
**the same is used for: EPS, PE ratio, ROE, Valuation**
#### <font style=color:magenta>EPS in RS</font>
- Known as **Earnings per equity share**. Iska matlab hai ki ek share pr company ne kitna kamaya, after calculating the net profit. Formula simple hai ki total profit ko agar total share se divide kar denge to nikal jayga ki Earning kitna hua ek share pr.
- Remember ki ye amout ka matlab ye nai hai ki aapko wo utna rupya milega. ye batata hai ki company ne kamaya utna rupya ek share pr. Hame jo paisa millega wo hamare share k badhne ya ghatne pr aayega ya fir company dividend degi to aayega. 
- Ab ye jitna higher hota hai utna badhiya hota hai, kyunki isse ye pata chalta hai ki bhai company jyada kama rhi hai tabhi to EPS itna high hai. agar kamayegi nai to EPS niche a jayega.
- Profit growth is useless if EPS doesn‚Äôt grow. So lets's say some company is earning good but at the same time they are also dilluting their share equity. This will lead to lower EPS because number of shares badh gaye to EPS badh gaya. and EPS nai badhta hai to profit k badhne se koi khash fayda nai hone wala hai.
- Agar EPS ka percentage me nikal kar aur profit growth ka percent se compare kare aur agar ye below scenarios aate hain to ye observation hai
	- Agar EPS % > Profit growth % == > iska matlab hai ki company ne kuch shares buyback kare hain aur profit waise hi badha hai lekin number of shares ghat gaye to EPS badh gaya more than profit. ye ek bahut hi acha bat hai.
	- Profit growth agar EPS se jyada bada hai to matlab ki company ne naye share issue kare hain, basically dilution kare.
	- Profit grows but EPS reduced ya negative me chala gaya.. to aise me this is very bad, kyunki company lagataar dillution kar rhi apne stock ka.
- SO best case scenario yehi hai ki ya to EPS profit growth se bada hoye ya fir atleast aas pass me to rhe.
#### <font style=color:magenta>DIVIDEND PAYOUT</font>
Percentage of profit paid as dividend.
- High-growth companies ‚Üí low dividend
- Mature companies ‚Üí high dividend
Neither is bad by default.

NOTE:- agar net profit sahi nai baith raha hai aur depreciation badhta jaa raha hai to bahut jyada chances hai ki company loan leki ya funds raise karenge share bech kar ya promoters apna share pledge karenge.

## <u>Cash Flow Analysis</u>
Cash flow tells us:-
‚úÖ Is real money coming into the company?  
‚úÖ Can it survive without loans?  
‚úÖ Is growth self-funded or debt-funded?  
‚úÖ Are profits genuine or accounting only?

Kai bar kya hota hai ki company ka profit high hai, aur growth v badhiya hai lekin Cash flow negative me hai ya kam hai, iska matlab hai ki company k pass jo profit hai wo bss kagaz pr hi hai, actual me wo profit convert nai ho raha hai cash me.
#### <font style=color:magenta>CASH FROM OPERATING ACTIVITY</font>
- Cash generated from **core business operations**. this includes **Cash received from customers, Cash paid to suppliers, Salaries, Power cost, Working capital changes**
- Kisi company ka financial statement me jo number bracket me milta hai wo negative number hua aur positive hua to bina bracket k hoga.
- matlab apne operating activity se company ne kitna cash generate kar paa rhi hai, and agar ye badhta hai to company ka acha growth show karta hai. Its a good sign.
- Ab agar CFO ko hum profit se compare karen aur dono me difference jyada hai to matlab hai ki company apne profit ko cash me convert nai kar paa rhi hai. aur ye problematic hai.
- Like company ka profit agar 100 cr hai aur CFO 80-90 cr hai to thik hai but agar CFO 50cr hai to problem hai. kyunki fir company survive kaise karegi. means ki company kharcha kar k maal bana rhi hai, bech v rhi hai but maal ko banate rhene k liye paisa aana v. to chahiye na, ab aayega hi nai to kaise banayengi companies mall?
- Aur agar company ka CFO negative me hai to matlab aur gadbadi hai.
***GOOD SIGN***
 this should be Higher than net profit. 
 formula:- **CFO > net profit.**
#### <font style=color:magenta>CASH FROM INVESTING ACTIVITY</font>
- Cash used for **long-term assets**
- Like **New factories, Machinery, Solar manufacturing plants, Land, Equipment, Subsidiaries**
- Ye agar negative hai to badhiya hai, wo ye show karta hai ki company investing for growth. and if positive hai to matlab hai ki company is selling assets.
- Lekin ek case me negative hona v bad hai, and wo hai ki **CFO weak hai, Debt is exploding, no revenue growth**
#### <font style=color:magenta>CASH FROM FINANCING ACTIVITY</font>
Money coming from:
- Loans
- Equity issue
- IPO proceeds
- Preference shares
Means company is raising money via these methods. Kya ye bura hai??
--> Well it depends, Financing cash used for "Paying losses, paying salaries" is bad. But Financing cash used for "New factories, Expansion, Capacity growth" is good.

this is good until company is under growth or expansion. But long term me CFO hi company k growth ko fund karna chahiye.

After all of these see the net Cash flow, if that is increasing over time then it means company is growing.
