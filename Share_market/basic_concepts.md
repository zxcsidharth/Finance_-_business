### TERMS:-
- ***OWNERS EQUITY***:- this is also called as Net Equity. 
  Net Equity = Total assets - Borrowings( jo karz ya kisi se liya gaya ho)
  Borrowings can be termed as liabilities. jo ki liye gaye loans ya paise hote hai. 
  aur agar koi business band ho jata hai to uss case me sare liabilities ko clear karna padta hai owners ko.
  
- ***PROMOTERS***:- a group of people or single person who started the company and want to promote it going ahead.
- ***PROTECTION AGAINST DEFAULTS***:-  if some company gets bankrupt then SEBI will ensure that the shareholders gets the money. By selling the asset of the company. (there are various rules that who will be the first people to get the money if a company gets defaulted)
- ***IPO***:- initial public offer, jab kisi company ko first time market se fund raise karna hota hai to uss time company IPO nikalte hain. So companies ko paisa isi se milta hai bad me NSE, BSE se share buy/sell hota hai usse company ko kuch paisa nai milta hai.
- ***FPO***:- Follow-on public offer. jab company ko market se aur paise chahiye hota hai to uss time companies FPO nikalte hain, kyunki company k pass to paisa bss IPO se hi jata hai bad me secondary market se company ko paisa nai milta hai.
- ***PRIMARY MARKET***:- where company directly sells the share to the investor but SEBI will also be there as the sole authority.
- ***SECONDARY MARKET***:-  Share market, like NSE BSE.
  ![[Pasted image 20241128103334.png]]
         this is the diagram to see how primary and secondary market runs.

- ***LISTING ON AN EXCHANGE***:- jab hum koi share kharid bech karte hain to uss case me to wo hamare demat account me list hota hai.. usi ko listing kehte hain.
- ***UNDERWRITERS***:- Wo financial institutions jo kisi company k sara financial details read karne k bad SEBI ya required person ko batati hain ki unka kitna ka valuation hai aur agar company ko IPO release karna hai to per share wo kitna rakhenge price.
- ***FIXED PRICE ISSUE:-*** IPO k time pr jab company fixed price rakh deti hai to usko ye kehte hain
- ***BOOK BUILD ISSUE:-*** company ek fixed price na deti hai instead ki wo range de deti hai like 200 - 220 k bich me per share price hona chahiye. jyadatar yehi wala issue market me release hota hai. 
- ***MARKET VALUE:-*** jo share ka current price hai usi ko market value bolte hain. and wo har roj change hote rehta hai.
- ***FV***:- when used in the context of share then its called as "Face value" but when used in context of the money then it is called as "future value". So face value is nothing but jab share ka IPO hone ka bad jo v uska initial price tha usi ko face value kehte hain. uske bad fir chahe jitna badhe ya ghate usko hum face value nai bolenge.
- ***DIVIDEND***:- Jo company ne profit kamaya usko shaare share holder me band diya gaya. sabko kuch kuch percentage millega like 5-10%.
- ***CORPORATE ACTIONS:-*** Matlab uss company k board of directory me kya kya action liya jata hai usi ko corporate actions kehte hain. so jab hum kisi share ka fundamental analysis kare to ye v dekhna jaruri hai, kyunki isse ye pata chalega ki company ne kya kya kiya hai apne shareholder ka, aur usse ye v pata chalega ki company apne shareholder k liye kitna serious hai.
- ***TURNOVER:-*** jo company ka total sales hota hai usi ko turnover kehte hain.
- ***TOPLINE:-*** turnover ko hi topline growth v bolte hain, so hame kisi share ko analyse karne me ye v dekhna hota hai ki company ka topline growth kaisa hai. sales badh rhe hain ya nai. **_Sometimes kisi company me aisa v hota hai ki sales to badh raha hai but profit margin ghatata jaa raha hai. to waha hame thora deep proper analysis karna hoga._**
- ***BOTTOMLINE:-*** Profit after tax ko bottomline bolte hain, so sometimes hame ye v dhyan rakhna hoga ki kai baar company ka profit to hua hai 100 cr but uske pass abhi 10 crore aaye nai hain. to yaha pr bottomline growth 90 crore ka hi hoga.

## <u>Difference between Intrinsic value and Market value.</u>

**Intrinsic value:-** (IV)
- Means fair/real value of a share.
- Hamesa share intrinsic value pr ya usse kam pr hi kharide

#### <u>Intrinsic Value Calculation</u>
- ***Personal Perception:-*** ye tab kaam aata hai jab hame jankari behtar ho aur hamare pass info ho.
- ***Compare with other companies*** :- it means usi sector k other stocks se compare karna like koi other company k stocks jo ki badh rhe ho kafi tezzi se, ya uss sector ko kuch special chutt milla ho. ya kisi tarah ka koi baat hai kya jiske wajah se share bss ek ka badhta jaa raha hai aur kisi ka nai badh raha hai.
- ***Discounted cash flow modal***:- this is also known as "Conservative approach"  jaise ki hame kisi given principle or 5% ka CAGR nikalna hai to hum compounding calculator ka use karte hain. so usi tarah agar hmko future value koi bata dega aur intrest bata dega to hum discouted value nikalte hain ki agar itna future me dega aur itne % ka growth deta hai  to matlab ki kitna rupya ka initial amount hoga after passing X year (i mean investement amount)
- Matlab ek taraf se compounding ka reverse hai ye.
- To online agar directly mai search karunga ki "discounted cash flow calculator" dhundh kar isse hame share ka intrinsic value ka pata chal jayega.

**Why this is important to check this?**
 - Because kai bar companies ya bade bade traders jhuthe news faila dete hain taki share price shoot kar jaye aur fir ya to wo company FPO launch kare ya jyada se jyada investor ko attract karne k liye ye sab v kiya jata hai.
- So kabhi v share buy karne se pehle uska intrinsic value jarur nikalen, agar wo overvalued hai to usme investement nai karna hai aur wo pakka kisi din apne original value pr aayega.
- agar undervalued hai hum kharid sakte hain.
- also before buying thora sa trends v dekhna jaruri hai like uss sector ki aur companies ka kya performance hai aur agar koi companies ka trends ulta chal raha ho to iska matlab kuch to gadbad hai.

#### <u>How to find undervalued stock by using P/E ? </u>
Uss company ko kisi aur company se compare karne se badhiya hai ki usko usik P/E se compare karo, 1yr, 3 yr, 5 yr ka median kitna hai, aur agar uska abhi ka p/e kitna hai. agar uska current p/e kam hai company k average P/e se to fir uske technical aur fundamentals ko check kar k usme position bana sakta hu.

***I can use same strategy for selling also:-*** Like koi stock agar le rakha hu mai aur uska P/E kafi badh gaya hai aur technicals v sale karne ko dikha rhe hain usko bech sakta hu. isse ye pata chal jayega ki stock kitna overvalue hai. aur overvalue stock ka aaj na kal girna hi padta hai.

#### <u>Terminologies</u>

***AMH***:- After market hours , ye kab hota hai?? ye hota hai 4pm from previous day to 9:00 am in next day.

***AMO***:- After market orders. (isme hum buy ya sell v kar sakte hain) jaise ki jo working people hain wo rat me hi order place kar dete hain.

***LTP***:- Last traded price bolte hain isko. iska matlab jab 3:30 pr market close hua tha tab jo last price tha uss share ka usi ko bolte hain LTP.

ek example hai ki agar koi share ka LTP 40 rs ho to agle din 9 bje iska dam dusra hoga kyunki post market order lagenge to iska dam kuch ghatega ya badhega. aur fir usi share ka dam 9:15 k baad aur badh jayega ya ghat jayega kyunki in the pre market session brokers log trader karte hain.

So now why Brokers trade but not investor??

-> Because market k volatility ko maintain karne k liye SEBI ne ye rula banaya hai. taki koi share ko jitna girna ya badhna rhega to wo badh kar ya ghat kar apne normal state me chala jayega because till then brokers trade kar k uss share ka jo hona hoga wo kar denge to jab normal aadmi k hath me aayega tab bahut jyada volatility nai rhega.

***GAP UP***:- Kal k market ka close hone k price se aaj market khulne k bad share me uchal dikhe agar to usko Gap up kehte ain, kyunki wo share opening hi hua hai higher price se because of pre market orders.

***GAP DOWN***:- Kal k market ka close hone k price se aaj market khulne k bad share me downfall dikhe agar to usko Gap down kehte ain, kyunki wo share opening hi hua hai lower price se compared to previous day closing price. because of pre market orders.

***DIVIDEND YIELD***:- ye percentage me hota hai aur iska matlab hai ki company ne itne percent ka dividend diya hai per share.

#### <u>Types of Orders</u>

***STOP LOSS***:- matlab ki loss me jab ho tab kitna loss me bech sakta hu to uske liye hum stop loss lagate hain usse kya hoga ki hamara share loss hoga to uss particular amount pr aate hi bikk jayega agar stop loss order ko touch kiya hai.

***TRIGGER PEICE***:- suppose hamara stock ka price hai 1000 aur maine stop loss lagaya 990 ka with 1% loss margin. so kai baar kya hota hai ki price jab bahut tezzi se girta hai to stop loss me sell nai hota hai. to uss case me hum trigger price lagate hain nearbuy to stop loss price like yaha pr iss cas eme 993 pr laga denge. so now jaise hi itna price pahunchega to kya hoga ki order book se nikal  trade book me chala jayega. aur fir jo v market me price millega uspr ye bik jayega. ye wait nai karega STOP LOSS ppr jane ka.

***IOC***:- Immediate or cancel order. jaise order **dete** samay maine dala hai ki 708.30 ka to iska matlab ki ye itne price pr abhi do ya rehne do. to basically ye market price ko dekh kar dala jata hai..to jo market price hai usi k around me set hota hai. this is similar to market orders but market orders me slippage ho sakta hai aur yaha pr slippage ka koi chance nai hai.

#### <u>SEBI action to prevent stock manipulation</u>

jab koi share bahut tezi se upar jaa raha ho to uss case me sebi ne apne side se har share k liye limit laga rakha hai ki ek share kitna uppar jaa sakta hai, so how does this limit works??

Lets say agar koi share bahut tezzi se upar jane laga to ek time pr SEBI kya karegi ki uss company me koi v trade karna band kar degi for 45 min (pause). uss dauran koi v na bech payega aur na hi kharid payega.

ab 45 Min baad fir se resume karenge SEBI wale fr 30 min, aur dekhenge ki kya share fir se bahut tezi se badhne laga to uss case me kya hoga ki fir iss share ko pure din k liye trading band kar denge, even within 30 min, (matlab ki dusri baar me 30 min paar hone ka wait nai karenge, balki turant rok diya jayega) and wo price jo touch kara hai share ne band hone se pehle usko bolte hain **UPPER CIRCUIT** aur same agar sahre gir raha hai to hoa aur jo uss range ka last price touch ho jayega to usko bolenge **LOWER CIRCUIT** 
![[Pasted image 20241202095406.png]]

Matlab ki ek din me ek share ek range se jyada nai badh sakta hai aur na hi ghat sakta hai.

Now Question is ki **iss range ko kaise aur kaun decide karta hai?**
So ye SEBI ne right diya hai BSE and NSE ko ki aap apne andar listed companies ka ek range decide kar k uspr limit apply kariye.

So Allowable % of circuit  (UPPER/LOWER)
2%, 5%, 10%, 20%, 40%. of Last day traded price. 

ye depend karta hai company kis nature ki hai, agar kuch aise sector ki hogi jaha volatility jyada hoga to waha limit thora jyada rhega aur share stable type ki hai to waha range limit kam rhega. 

iss ekya hoga ki investors k sharee paise dubenge nai maximum uss ange me hi paise dub sakte hain.

***EXCEPTION***:- jo companies Future&option me v trade karti hain wo companies k liye upper circuit aur lower circuit bss ek formality hai because wo jaise hi uss limit ko cross karenge waise hi turant unka naya limit unlock ho jayega. TO iss tarah ki companies me abhi v stock manipulation chal rhe hain, like ring trading and all.

ye kya hota hai ki kuch bade brokers suppose 10 tho honge jo ek dusre ko jante honge aur unke bich me ye setting hoga ki hum sab ek dusre ko hi share kharid bech karenge aur per share 20 rs ka profit rakhnege suppose.

   To uss case me person B hoga wo same 100000 share ka order place kar dega with 100rs per share.

4. Aur Fir aise kar k sabka profit nikal jayega aur sare brokers ne karib karib 20 lakh ka profit kama liya.
